Our Departments

Biological Sciences

​The Biological Sciences program provides a good introduction to living matter through courses in biological cell sciences and biophysics. The program consists of two academic majors of integrated courses and includes lectures, scientific seminars, and practical training in the laboratory. Each course provides a full presentation of the relevant scientific subject.

BioEngineering

​Bioengineering focuses on the development of engineering concepts, principles, and methods and their applications to biological systems. It aims to model, monitor and treat disturbances and obstacles that affect living organisms, in addition to developing algorithms that help understand biological systems and improve their performance through engineering living systems.

Computer Science

The Computer Science program aims to train students to create computer models and apply different computer methods in many fields. The Computer Science program includes six core specializations, through which students reach the fullest extent of computer technologies.

Applied Physics

The program aims to design innovative solutions for many applied physics disciplines, such as optics, photonics, semiconductor devices, quantum electronics, and new materials for energy applications.
